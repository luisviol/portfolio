WhyX - Circular economy


WhyX, an innovative app, transforms consumption with its circular economy model, facilitating seamless product sharing. Through diverse exchanges, it promotes sustainable living and community-driven responsibility, fostering a greener future.

Role: UX Designer, Visual Designer

The Problem

As a lone wolf navigating the complexities of the digital realm, I faced a significant challenge—maintaining consistency across design and development. My products suffered from fragmented experiences, prolonged development timelines, and a lack of unified branding. The absence of a comprehensive design system was a roadblock that hindered me from delivering top-tier user experiences.

Goal

I embarked on a personal mission—to craft a design system that could conquer these challenges head-on. My goals for the StreamlineUX project were crystal clear:

Boost Consistency:

I aimed to establish a uniform visual and interactive language across all my products

Enhance Efficiency:

My objective was to streamline the design and development processes, reducing both time and effort.

Foster Collaboration:

I sought to bridge the gap between my design and development roles, creating a seamless workflow.

Centralize Guidance:

I aimed to provide myself with comprehensive guidelines for branding, UI components, and interactions.

Initial Process

My journey kicked off with a thorough analysis of my own design and development workflows. Identifying pain points, inconsistencies, and areas for improvement was my starting point. Self-reflective workshops granted me invaluable insights into the unique challenges I faced as a solo creator. This early phase laid the foundation for what would evolve into StreamlineUX.

Design Process

Ideation: As a designer and developer, I crafted atomic blueprints, which later evolved into larger components. High-Fidelity: My blueprints transformed into high-fidelity designs, ready for the development phase. Handoff (To Myself): I took over the role of a developer using these atomic components, expediting development. Reviews (With Myself): Self-conducted review sessions ensured my designs adhered to the system.
